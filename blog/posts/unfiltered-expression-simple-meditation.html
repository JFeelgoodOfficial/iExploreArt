Unfiltered Expression: The Raw Intimacy of JFeelgood's 'A Simple Meditation'
"My idea was to escape a sense of contrived concept, while keeping my presence in pure unadulterated raw, honest expression. As true to a mirror for my spirit's state as possible."
— JFeelgood

I kept my paint time down to 10 minutes, no more. Whatever I could do in that time is what the painting would be. The absence of paintbrushes permits me to be more intimate with emerging expression — my only tools for application were the paint tubes and my hands.

It began with A Simple Meditation, but it felt so good and right that a three-piece series came of it. Ronin and Monarch followed to complete the series.

Analysis of "A Simple Meditation"

A Simple Meditation is an evocative acrylic on canvas piece measuring 24×36×2 inches. This painting captures the essence of introspection and inner peace through its abstract representation of a solitary figure. The use of bold, gestural strokes in yellow, white, and blue creates a dynamic interplay of colour and form, conveying a sense of both energy and calm.

The central figure, rendered in an almost primitive style, appears deep in thought, holding a black cup — a possible symbol of contemplation or solitude. The abstract background, with its erratic lines and patches of colour, suggests a chaotic mind seeking order through meditation.

On the medium: The raw, unrefined marks made by hand add an element of authenticity and emotional depth that brushwork simply cannot replicate. The intimacy of touch changes what the paint can say.

The result invites viewers to reflect on their own states of mind — and the quiet pursuit of tranquility amidst life's chaos.
