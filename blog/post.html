Unfiltered Expression: A Reading of A Simple Meditation

A closer reading of "A Simple Meditation," framed around introspection, raw gesture, and the quiet power of honest mark-making.

J
JFeelgood March 15, 2026  ·  6 min read
A Simple Meditation

A Simple Meditation — Original painting by JFeelgood, 2026

There's a version of abstraction that performs. It announces itself. It arrives with theory already attached, with a vocabulary borrowed from art history, with the sense that the work is speaking toward an audience rather than from a person. That version isn't what interests me.

A Simple Meditation started differently. It started with nothing to prove.

"My idea was to escape a sense of contrived concept, while keeping my presence in pure unadulterated raw, honest expression."
— JFeelgood

What I Saw When I Looked

The work is built on gesture — broad, unguarded strokes that don't apologize for their direction. There's a warmth in the palette that isn't decorative. It's more like ambient light, the kind you notice only when it changes. The composition resists a single entry point, which I think is the point. You don't read it so much as you settle into it.

On looking: If you find yourself reaching for an interpretation immediately, try staying with the feeling first. The meaning in abstract work often arrives second.

On the Process Behind It

I was working through something that day — not a concept, just an internal pressure that needed a channel. The canvas became that channel. I wasn't steering toward a predetermined image. I was following the paint, adjusting, responding, staying honest about what each mark was doing.

That kind of process is uncomfortable at first. You don't have a plan to hide behind. Every decision is exposed. But that exposure is also what makes the finished work feel present — like it was actually made by someone, not constructed.

What Meditation Means Here

The title came after. I don't usually name work during the making. But looking back at it — the stillness in the gesture, the way it holds without tension — meditation felt accurate. Not in a spiritual performance sense. More like: this is what being present with paint looks like.
