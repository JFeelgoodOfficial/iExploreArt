About the work

Abstract painting is the place where I get to be most honest. There's no subject to hide behind, no story line to follow. Just paint, surface, and whatever I'm carrying into the studio that day.

The work isn't planned in advance. It's responsive — built from gesture, adjusted in real time, pushed until something true appears. That means some paintings arrive quickly and others take weeks of revision. I don't rush either kind.

My palette runs warm and textural. I'm interested in the difference between a mark that's placed and a mark that's made — and in keeping as many of the latter as I can.

About iExploreArt

iExploreArt started as a way to give the work a context it could live inside. Not just a gallery wall or a shop listing, but a place where someone could read about the thinking behind the painting — or the painting behind the thinking.

The site features original painting analysis, first-person studio writing, and artist spotlights. It's built for collectors, artists, and anyone who wants to slow down and look more carefully.

I write everything here myself. It's a personal blog, not a publication — which means it moves at the pace of the studio, not a content calendar.
